Signs You Might Need Professional Pest Control Service

Recognizing the early signs of a pest infestation can save you significant time, money, and stress in the long run. While a single ant or spider might not signal a major problem, persistent sightings or specific indicators should prompt you to consider professional help. Pests are often more adept than we are at hiding, and by the time you see significant signs, the infestation could be much larger than you realize. Acting quickly when you notice the first few warning signs is always the best approach to minimize the impact and cost of pest control. Pay close attention to any unusual activity or discoveries around your property. Here are some common signs that indicate it might be time to call Herriman Pest Pros:

Unusual Odors

Many pests leave behind distinct odors that can signal their presence, even if you don’t see them. A musty, unpleasant smell could indicate a rodent infestation, particularly in confined spaces like attics or crawl spaces. Cockroaches are also known to produce a detectable, oily odor, especially in larger infestations. If you notice persistent or unusual smells in your home or business that you can’t identify, it’s a good idea to investigate the possibility of a hidden pest problem. These odors can become more pronounced in areas with poor ventilation and can soak into porous materials over time, making them difficult to eliminate even after the pests are gone. Identifying the source of the odor is a key part of our inspection process.

Droppings or Fecal Matter

Finding small droppings or fecal matter is a clear and undeniable sign of pest activity. The appearance of the droppings can help identify the type of pest. Mouse droppings are typically small and pellet-shaped, while rat droppings are larger and more oblong. Cockroach droppings can resemble black pepper or coffee grounds. The location and quantity of droppings can also provide clues about the severity of the infestation and where the pests are most active. Discovering these signs in kitchen cabinets, pantries, basements, or attics should be a strong indicator to seek professional pest control assistance. Our technicians are experienced in identifying different types of pest droppings and can use this information to pinpoint the infestation’s source.

Physical Damage to Property

Pests can cause a variety of physical damage to your property. Rodents may gnaw on wires, wood, pipes, and insulation, creating fire hazards and compromising structure. Termites and carpenter ants tunnel through wood, weakening structural elements. Silverfish and moths can damage clothing, books, and wallpaper as they feed. Finding inexplicable holes in walls, damaged wiring, chewed-up materials, or trails of sawdust can all be indicators of a pest problem that requires professional attention. The extent of the damage can sometimes provide an estimate of how long the infestation has been present and how large the population might be. We can assess the damage during our inspection and advise on necessary repairs related to pest activity.

Visible Pest Sightings

While some pests are nocturnal or hide well, seeing pests during the day, or repeatedly seeing them at all, is a strong indication of an infestation. Whether you see ants marching in a line across your kitchen floor, notice a spider building webs in prominent locations, or catch a glimpse of a mouse scurrying across the room, these sightings should not be ignored. Increased frequency of sightings often suggests a growing population within your property. The type of pest you see, where you see it, and the time of day can all provide valuable information about the nature of the infestation. Our technicians will ask about your pest sightings during the initial consultation to help guide their inspection.

Unusual Sounds

Pests can produce a variety of sounds that can alert you to their presence. Scratching or scurrying noises in walls, attics, or ceilings, particularly at night, are often indicative of rodent activity. Buzzing or humming sounds could signal a bee or wasp nest. The sound of chewing or gnawing can also indicate the presence of certain pests. Paying attention to these unusual noises and trying to pinpoint their location can help identify the type of pest and where they are harbored within your property. Hearing these sounds can be unsettling and disruptive, especially during quiet periods, and should prompt you to investigate further or contact a professional.

Nesting Materials

Finding nesting materials in unusual places in your home or business is a sure sign of pest activity. Rodents, for example, will often gather materials like shredded paper, fabric, or insulation to build their nests in secluded areas such as attics, basements, or wall voids. Finding these collections of materials can help pinpoint the location of a nest and indicate a breeding population of pests on your property. The type of nesting material used can also sometimes provide a clue about the specific pest species involved. Removing nesting materials and addressing the pests is crucial for preventing further reproduction and infestation.

Finding Shed Skins or Wings

As some insects grow, they shed their exoskeletons, or nymphs may shed their skins as they mature. Finding these discarded skins in areas like near baseboards, behind furniture, or in storage areas can be an indicator of an active or recent infestation. For example, finding small, translucent bed bug skins is a definitive sign of a bed bug problem. Discarded termite wings, often found on windowsills or near lights, are a key indicator of a termite swarm, suggesting a mature colony is nearby. Recognizing these subtle signs can be key to early detection and intervention, preventing the infestation from becoming more severe. Our technicians are trained to spot these less obvious signs of pest activity.

Chew Marks or Gnaw Marks

Pests like rodents are constantly gnawing to keep their teeth sharp and to access food or create pathways. Finding chew marks on electrical wires, wooden furniture, baseboards, cardboard boxes, or food packaging is a clear sign of rodent activity. The size and shape of the chew marks can even provide clues about whether you are dealing with mice or rats. These marks are not just unsightly; gnawed wires can create fire hazards, and damaged food packaging can lead to contamination. Inspecting areas where food is stored or where wires are present can help you identify this tell-tale sign of a rodent problem.

Live Pests Swarming

Seeing a large number of pests at once, or observing a swarm, is usually indicative of a significant infestation, often related to reproduction or migration. For example, a termite swarm, where winged termites emerge in large numbers, is a strong indicator of a mature termite colony nearby. Finding a large number of ants suddenly appearing in a specific area could also signal a growing colony. Witnessing live pests swarming is a sign that the infestation is well-established and requires immediate professional intervention. This is often the most alarming sign for property owners and typically prompts an urgent call for help.
