Targeted Treatment Protocols

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan designed to eliminate active termite colonies and prevent future infestations. Our protocols may involve a combination of methods, depending on the species of termite, the severity of the infestation, and the specific characteristics of your property. Common treatment methods we utilize include:

Liquid Barrier Treatments

Creating a protective barrier around your property's foundation is a highly effective method for preventing termites from entering. We apply a specialized liquid termiticide to the soil surrounding your home or business. This creates a treated zone that termites cannot pass through. This method is particularly effective for subterranean termites, which build their colonies in the soil.

Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer a sustainable and environmentally conscious approach to termite control. These systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insect growth regulator or toxicant that termites consume and share with their colony members. This gradually eliminates the entire colony, including the queen. Baiting systems can be particularly effective for long-term control and monitoring.

Wood Treatments

For areas where termites are actively feeding on wood, or for preventative measures on susceptible wooden structures, we may apply specialized wood treatments. These treatments can include termiticides applied directly to the wood or borate-based products that seep into the wood fibers, making it repellent and toxic to termites. This is often used in crawl spaces, attics, and other areas with exposed wood.

Our team will explain the recommended treatment plan in detail, outlining the process, the products used, and what you can expect. We prioritize using the most effective and appropriate methods for your specific situation, ensuring the safety of your family, pets, and the environment.

Understanding Termites: Common Problems and Signs

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation is crucial for early detection and intervention. The sooner you identify a problem, the less damage termites can inflict on your property. Here are some common issues and signs to look out for, along with how our expert termite treatment in Herriman, UT can help:

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundation

Mud tubes are one of the most visible signs of subterranean termites. These small tunnels, made of soil and wood, allow termites to travel safely between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood). Finding mud tubes on your exterior walls, foundation, or even interior walls is a strong indicator of termite activity. Ignoring these tubes allows the infestation to continue and worsen. Our inspection will locate all active and inactive mud tubes, and our treatment will target the colony responsible for creating them, effectively shutting down their access routes.

Damaged Wood Structures

Termites eat wood from the inside out, making the damage often invisible until it's significant. Look for wood that sounds hollow when tapped, is soft or easily punctured, or appears to have tiny tunnels or galleries within it. Swollen floors or ceilings, distorted doorframes, and walls that feel spongy can also be signs of termite damage. Early detection of damaged wood allows us to focus our treatment on the affected areas and prevent further destruction. We can also advise on necessary repairs or structural reinforcement.

Termite Swarmers

Swarmers are winged termites that emerge from mature colonies, typically in the spring, to reproduce and establish new colonies. If you see swarmers inside your home, especially near windows or light sources, it's a definite sign of an existing termite infestation within your property. Finding discarded wings, which they shed after swarming, is also a key indicator. Seeing swarmers means the colony is well-established. Our treatment will target the parent colony, preventing further swarming events and eliminating the infestation at its source.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

As termites consume the wood within your home's structure, it weakens the integrity of beams, joists, and other support structures. This damage can manifest as sagging floors or ceilings. This is a serious sign of significant termite damage and requires immediate professional attention. Our comprehensive inspection will assess the extent of the structural damage, and our aggressive treatment plan will eliminate the termites and prevent further deterioration. We can also recommend structural engineers for necessary repairs.

Maze-like Patterns in Wood

If you find wood that appears to have intricate, maze-like patterns carved into it, this is a clear sign of termite feeding activity. These patterns are the galleries and tunnels that termites excavate as they consume the wood. This damage can be found in structural wood members, furniture, or even wooden decorative items. Discovering these patterns confirms the presence of active termites within your property. Our targeted treatment will focus on the areas where this damage is identified, eliminating the termites actively feeding on your wood.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not live in the soil. They live and feed within the wood itself. One sign of a drywood termite infestation is the presence of frass, which are small, pellet-like droppings that termites push out of the tiny holes they create in the wood. The appearance of frass near wooden structures or furniture suggests a drywood termite presence. Our inspection and treatment methods are also effective against drywood termites, utilizing specialized techniques to target colonies within the wood.

Recognizing these signs allows you to take prompt action. Don't wait for the damage to become extensive. Contact Herriman Pest Pros at the first sign of a potential termite problem.
